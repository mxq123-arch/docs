By default, your Mintlify documentation is accessible at an auto-generated subdomain. Hosting on a custom domain — such as `docs.yourcompany.com` — gives your docs a professional URL that matches your brand and improves SEO. Mintlify handles TLS certificate provisioning automatically once your DNS records are in place.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add your domain in the dashboard">
    Navigate to the [Custom domain setup](https://app.mintlify.com/settings/deployment/custom-domain) page in your dashboard. Enter your domain name (for example, `docs.example.com` or `www.example.com`) and click **Add domain**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a CNAME record">
    On your domain provider's DNS settings page, create a new CNAME record pointing your subdomain to Mintlify:

    ```
    CNAME | docs | cname.mintlify.builders
    ```

    Each domain provider has a different interface for adding DNS records. Refer to your provider's documentation for specific instructions.

    <Warning>
      Do not add or change your `CNAME` until both verification `TXT` records show as verified in your dashboard (indicated by a green check mark). Switching `CNAME` too early commonly breaks HTTPS until certificate provisioning finishes.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add the verification TXT records">
    After you add a custom domain, the dashboard displays two `TXT` records that you must create at your DNS provider:

    ```
    TXT | _acme-challenge.<your-domain> | <value shown in your dashboard>
    TXT | _cf-custom-hostname.<your-domain> | <value shown in your dashboard>
    ```

    - The `_acme-challenge` record authorizes Let's Encrypt to issue a TLS certificate for your domain.
    - The `_cf-custom-hostname` record verifies that you control the domain.

    The dashboard polls DNS in the background and marks each record with a green check once it confirms the expected value. Allow a short time for propagation after saving your records.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Wait for DNS propagation and TLS provisioning">
    DNS changes typically take 1–24 hours to propagate globally, though in rare cases it can take up to 48 hours. Use a tool like [DNSChecker](https://dnschecker.org) to verify your records are resolving correctly.

    Once your DNS records resolve, your documentation is first accessible over HTTP. Mintlify provisions a free TLS certificate via Let's Encrypt automatically, and HTTPS becomes available once the certificate is issued — typically within a few hours. Certificates are renewed automatically before expiration.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  For zero-downtime migration of an existing domain, publish the verification `TXT` records first and wait until they show as verified and TLS has pre-provisioned before pointing your `CNAME` at Mintlify.
</Tip>

## Cloudflare-proxied domains

If your domain is already proxied through Cloudflare (the proxy status shows an orange cloud icon), the standard pre-validation flow does not work. Cloudflare's proxy prevents the `TXT` records from showing as verified until traffic for the hostname already routes to Mintlify.

For Cloudflare-proxied domains, follow these steps instead:

1. Add the verification `TXT` records at your DNS provider.
2. Update your `CNAME` record to point to `cname.mintlify.builders` **without** waiting for the `TXT` records to show as verified.
3. Wait for verification and TLS provisioning to complete. Your site may briefly serve an invalid certificate during provisioning.

If you need zero downtime during migration, set the `CNAME` record's proxy status to **DNS only** (gray cloud) temporarily. This allows the standard pre-validation flow to complete before you switch traffic.

### Cloudflare SSL/TLS settings

If Cloudflare is your DNS provider, you must configure two additional settings:

- Set the **SSL/TLS encryption mode** to **Full (strict)**.
- Disable **Always Use HTTPS** in your Edge Certificates settings. Cloudflare's automatic HTTPS redirect blocks Let's Encrypt from validating your domain during certificate provisioning.

## Set a canonical URL

After your domain is live, set a canonical URL in your `docs.json` so search engines index your preferred domain and avoid duplicate content issues:

```json
{
  "seo": {
    "metatags": {
      "canonical": "https://docs.yourcompany.com"
    }
  }
}
```

Replace `https://docs.yourcompany.com` with your actual custom domain.

## Retry validation

If your domain remains pending after you add the verification `TXT` records, you can retry validation manually:

1. Navigate to the [Custom domain setup](https://app.mintlify.com/settings/deployment/custom-domain) page in your dashboard.
2. Find your pending custom domain.
3. Click **Retry validation**.

Only retry after confirming your DNS records are correct. Repeated retries with incorrect records do not speed up the validation process.

## CAA records

If your domain uses CAA (Certification Authority Authorization) records, you must explicitly authorize Let's Encrypt to issue certificates. Add the following CAA record to your DNS settings:

```
0 issue "letsencrypt.org"
```

Without this record, certificate provisioning will fail if your domain has existing CAA restrictions.

<Note>
  Mintlify reserves the `/.well-known/acme-challenge` path for certificate validation. Do not configure redirects or rewrites for this path. If you have existing rules that affect this path, certificate provisioning will fail.
</Note>
